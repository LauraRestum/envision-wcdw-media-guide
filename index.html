How to talk about Envision

The organization behind the Walk

Refer to the organization as Envision. For market context say “Envision in North Texas” or “North Texans,” and use “Envision, Dallas Campus” for the location — never “Envision Dallas” as the organization's name. The fundraising entity is Envision's Dallas Foundation (with the apostrophe).

Language that respects the community

Say this, never that

The Walk celebrates independence and agency. Keep the framing human-first, never clinical, and never pity- or deficit-based.

✓ Say this

  • people who are blind or have low vision — the only approved way to describe the community
  • Envision as the organization; “Envision in North Texas” for market context
  • Envision, Dallas Campus for the location
  • Envision's Dallas Foundation — with the apostrophe
  • “operates across Wichita and the Dallas area” when describing Envision's footprint
  • Lead with independence and local impact; center agency, skills, and community
  • One primary call to action per asset, with an action verb (“Register today,” “Start a team”)
  • Plain words: use (not “utilize”), improve (not “optimize”)

✕ Never this

  • “visually impaired,” “the blind,” or “BVI” — retired; spell out the approved phrase instead
  • “Envision Dallas” as the organization's name or market identity
  • Retired program names: Envision Xpress, College Success Program, Talent Network
  • Making the Zoo the headline — it's the venue, not the story
  • Pity or deficit framing (“suffers from,” “victim of,” “confined to”)
  • “Every dollar directly supports…” unless specifically approved
  • “Click here” as a link or button label
  • Em dashes in campaign copy — use a comma instead

Brand basics

Palette, type & logo

Click any swatch to copy its hex value. The event logo (in the asset library) goes on materials unmodified, at 100% opacity, with clear space around it.

Typography & style

  • Montserrat is the campaign typeface; ExtraBold for headlines.
  • Headlines and buttons are written in sentence case — never all caps, never Title Case.
  • High contrast, legible type on every graphic; caption all video.
  • In an email, hyperlink header banners to whitecanedaywalk.com so the whole image clicks through to registration.
For broadcast & radio

Ready-to-read PSA scripts

Approved :10 and :30 spots. One ask in the voiceover — register. Sponsor recognition belongs on the end card only. Keep the voice warm, calm, grounded, and hopeful — human first, not clinical.
